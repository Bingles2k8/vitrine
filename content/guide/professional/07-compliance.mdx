## Collections compliance

The **Compliance** section helps you build and maintain the documentation that responsible museum practice requires. Vitrine tracks five key areas across your entire collection and gives you a live score: the percentage of your objects with complete documentation.

The compliance dashboard shows you the overall score at the top, and below it a breakdown by category, showing how many objects have each piece of information recorded. Clicking any category reveals the specific objects that are missing that data.

<ComplianceDashboardMockup />

The five areas tracked:

- **Provenance**: the ownership and custody history of each object, from its origin to the present day
- **Acquisition method**: how the object entered your collection (purchase, donation, bequest, transfer, field collection, or unknown)
- **Conservation condition**: the current physical state of the object, with a condition rating and descriptive notes
- **Rights and reproduction**: copyright status and any licensing restrictions on reproducing images of the object
- **Loan agreements**: documentation for any objects currently on loan in or out of your institution

<Callout type="info">
  Vitrine's compliance tools are designed around the UK's Spectrum collections management standard and align with the documentation requirements for Arts Council England museum accreditation.
</Callout>

## Checking your compliance score

<Steps title="How do I see my overall compliance score?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Compliance** in the left sidebar. (If you don't see Compliance listed, it's a Professional+ feature; check your current plan in Settings.)</Step>
  <Step number={2}>The overview page shows your score as a percentage (e.g. "87% complete") with a progress bar. This represents the proportion of your total collection that has all five compliance fields documented.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Below the score, each of the five categories shows its own completion rate (e.g. "Provenance documented: 131/142"). A green tick means the category is complete for all objects; an amber warning icon flags categories with gaps.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Click any category to see exactly which objects are missing that information. Click an object's title to jump directly to its detail page and fill in the missing data.</Step>
</Steps>

## Recording provenance

Provenance is the documented history of an object: who owned it, where it was, and how it passed from hand to hand before arriving in your collection. Good provenance records are essential for due diligence, especially around culturally sensitive objects.

<ProvenanceTabMockup />

<Steps title="How do I record the provenance of an object?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object's detail page from the Objects list.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click the **Provenance** tab (the second tab along, after Details).</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Click **+ Add provenance entry**. Each entry represents one stage in the object's history.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Enter a **date or date range** for this stage (e.g. "1847" or "1962–1989"), a **description** of the ownership or custody at that time, and optionally the name of the person or institution involved.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Add as many entries as you have information for, building up a chronological chain from the object's origin to its entry into your collection.</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Even a partial provenance record is better than none. Record what you know with confidence, and use notes to flag gaps (e.g. "Ownership between 1910 and 1962 unverified"). A documented acknowledgement of uncertainty is more credible than a blank record.
</Callout>

## Recording acquisition method

<Steps title="How do I record how an object was acquired?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object's detail page and click the **Acquisition** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Choose the **Acquisition method** from the dropdown: Purchase, Donation, Bequest, Transfer, Field collection, or Unknown.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Enter the **Acquisition date** (even an approximate year is useful, e.g. "c. 1962").</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Add any relevant notes, for example the name of the vendor, the donor's connection to the object, or the provenance of the funds used for a purchase.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</Steps>

## Conservation condition

<Steps title="How do I log the condition of an object?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object and click the **Conservation** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Select a **condition rating** from the dropdown: Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor, or Critical.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Write descriptive **condition notes** in the text area. Be specific: describe any damage, staining, structural weakness, or previous repairs visible on the object. Future staff will rely on these notes to assess how condition has changed over time.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Record the **date of the assessment** so the notes can be put in context later.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>If you have a formal condition report document, attach it in the **Documents** tab for the complete record.</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</Steps>

## Rights and reproduction

<Steps title="How do I record the copyright status of an object?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object and click the **Rights** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Set the **Copyright status**: In copyright, Public domain, Unknown, or Orphan work (a work that is in copyright but whose rights holder cannot be found).</Step>
  <Step number={3}>If the object is in copyright, record the **rights holder** (the person or organisation that owns the copyright) and any **licence terms** you're aware of.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Add notes on any specific restrictions: for example, "Image may not be reproduced without written permission" or "Licenced for non-commercial use only."</Step>
  <Step number={5}>If you have a rights or licence agreement document, attach it in the **Documents** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</Steps>

## Loan agreements

<Steps title="How do I record a loan?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the relevant object and click the **Loans** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click **+ Add loan record**.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Specify the **Loan type**: Loan out (your object is at another institution) or Loan in (another institution's object is at yours).</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Enter the name of the **borrowing or lending institution**, the agreed **start and end dates** of the loan, and any special conditions.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Also update the object's **Status** to **On Loan** (this removes it from your public collection browser for the duration of the loan while keeping the record in your catalogue).</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Attach the signed loan agreement document in the **Documents** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={7}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  When a loan ends, remember to update both the object's status (back to On Display or In Storage) and add an end date to the loan record. This keeps your compliance dashboard accurate.
</Callout>
