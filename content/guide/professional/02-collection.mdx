## Collection management on Professional+

On Professional and above, the collection catalogue expands significantly. You can hold up to **5,000 objects on Professional**, **100,000 on Institution**, and **unlimited on Enterprise**. Each object supports up to **10 images**.

Beyond the larger capacity, Professional plans add several capabilities not available on Essentials plans:

- **Document storage**: attach PDFs, scans, and other files directly to object records (1 GB on Professional, 10 GB on Institution, unlimited on Enterprise)
- **Depositor tracking**: record donor details, entry method, GDPR consent status, and whether a receipt was issued
- **Extended metadata**: provenance chains, rights and reproduction status, conservation condition records, and loan agreement tracking
- **CSV bulk import**: populate your catalogue from a spreadsheet rather than adding objects one by one

## Adding and editing objects

Adding objects on Professional works the same way as on Essentials, with additional tabs visible on the object detail page for Provenance, Conservation, Rights, and Documents.

<ProObjectDetailMockup />

<Steps title="How do I add a new object?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Objects** in the sidebar, then click **+ Add object** in the top-right corner.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Fill in the **Title** (required) and as many other fields as you have: date, medium, dimensions, description, and accession number.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Upload up to **10 images** using the image panel. Drag to reorder them; the first image in the list becomes the primary display image shown in your catalogue and on your public site.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Use the **Provenance**, **Conservation**, and **Rights** tabs to fill in any compliance-related information you have at this stage. (See the Compliance section of this guide for full detail on each.)</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Set the **Status** and click **Save**. The object is live on your public site immediately if you chose On Display.</Step>
</Steps>

## Bulk importing from a spreadsheet

If you have an existing catalogue in a spreadsheet, you can import it directly rather than entering objects one by one. This is the fastest way to get a large collection into Vitrine.

<Steps title="How do I import objects from a CSV file?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Objects** in the sidebar, then click the **Import** button (the icon with an upward arrow in the toolbar above the list).</Step>
  <Step number={2}>A drawer will open. Click **Download CSV template** to get a spreadsheet with the exact columns Vitrine expects. Open it in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Fill in your collection data using the template as a guide. The only required column is **Title**; you can leave any other column blank if you don't have that information.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Save your completed spreadsheet as a CSV file, then drag it onto the import drawer or click to upload it.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Vitrine will validate every row and show you a preview table. Any rows with errors are highlighted in red with an explanation of the problem.</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Fix any errors in your spreadsheet and re-upload, or dismiss individual problem rows and import the rest. When you're satisfied, click **Confirm import**.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  For large collections, consider importing in batches of 500–1,000 rows. It's easier to spot and fix errors in a smaller set, and you can review each batch in the catalogue before moving on to the next.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
  The CSV import creates new object records; it does not update existing ones. If you need to correct records you've already imported, edit them individually on their detail pages.
</Callout>

## Document attachments

Documents can be attached directly to any object record. Use this for acquisition receipts, condition reports, loan agreements, rights licences, insurance certificates, or any other supporting paperwork.

<ObjectWithDocumentsMockup />

<Steps title="How do I attach a document to an object?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object's detail page by clicking its title in the Objects list.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click the **Documents** tab near the top of the page (to the right of Details, Provenance, Conservation, and Rights).</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Click **+ Attach document**. Select the file from your computer (PDFs, Word documents, and image files are all supported).</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Give the document a short **label** that describes what it is, such as "Acquisition receipt 1962" or "Condition report 2023". This label is how you'll identify the document in the list later.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Click **Upload**. The document is stored securely against this object's record.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  All document storage counts against your plan's storage quota. You can see how much you've used at any time in **Settings → Plan**. If you're running low, you can upgrade to Institution for 10 GB, or delete documents no longer needed from individual object pages.
</Callout>

## Depositor and acquisition records

When objects enter your collection through donation, bequest, or purchase, you can record the full acquisition details (including depositor contact information and GDPR consent) directly on the object.

<Steps title="How do I record a depositor or acquisition details?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object's detail page.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click the **Acquisition** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Fill in the **Depositor name** and their contact details. You can also record the **Acquisition method** (Purchase, Donation, Bequest, Transfer, Field collection, or Unknown) and the exact acquisition date.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Check or uncheck the **GDPR consent given** checkbox to record whether the depositor consented to their information being stored.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Use the **Receipt issued** toggle to note whether a formal receipt was provided to the depositor.</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Attach any signed documentation (entry forms, receipts) in the **Documents** tab for a complete paper trail.</Step>
  <Step number={7}>Click **Save**.</Step>
</Steps>

## QR codes and object labels

Every object in Vitrine has a unique QR code that can be printed as a label. Scanning the code takes visitors or staff directly to the object's detail page, useful for gallery labels, storage tags, and loan paperwork.

<Steps title="How do I print a QR label for an object?">
  <Step number={1}>Open the object's detail page.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click the **QR label** button (the small QR icon in the top-right of the page, next to the status badge).</Step>
  <Step number={3}>A label preview opens. Choose your preferred label format (small tag, medium card, or A5 sheet).</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Click **Print** to send it to your printer, or **Download PDF** to save it for batch printing later.</Step>
</Steps>
