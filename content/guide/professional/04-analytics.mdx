## Analytics

The **Analytics** section shows you how people are finding and using your public collection website. All data is collected by Vitrine directly. No third-party cookies, no Google Analytics, no tracking scripts that put consent banners on your site.

<AnalyticsDashboardMockup />

At the top of the analytics page you'll find three headline numbers: **page views** (total number of individual page loads), **unique visitors** (distinct people, based on session data), and **average time on site**. Alongside each is a percentage showing the change from the previous equivalent period.

Below the headline numbers, a bar chart shows page views broken down by day across your selected date range. Use this to spot patterns: peaks after social media posts, dips during school holidays, the effect of a press mention.

Further down, you'll find:

- **Top objects**: the individual object pages that attracted the most visits
- **Traffic sources**: a breakdown showing whether visitors arrived directly (typing the URL or from a bookmark), via a search engine, or from a social media link

## Changing the date range

<Steps title="How do I view analytics for a specific time period?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Analytics** in the sidebar. The default view shows the last 7 days.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click the date range selector in the top-right corner of the page. A dropdown shows preset options: **Last 7 days**, **Last 30 days**, **Last 90 days**, and **Custom range**.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>To select a custom period, choose **Custom range**, then click a start date on the calendar, then click an end date. Click **Apply**.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>All numbers and charts on the page update to reflect your chosen period.</Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding your most-viewed objects

<Steps title="How do I see which objects are most popular?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Analytics** and scroll down past the bar chart to the **Top objects** section.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>This table shows individual object pages ranked by visit count for the selected period. The most-visited object appears at the top.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Click any object title in the list to jump directly to that object's detail page in the dashboard, useful if you want to add more information to a popular piece.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Your top objects list is one of the most actionable pieces of data in analytics. The objects people are seeking out are the ones worth investing more time in: better photos, richer descriptions, more provenance detail. A well-documented popular object reflects well on your whole collection.
</Callout>

## Understanding traffic sources

<Steps title="How do I see where my visitors are coming from?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Analytics** and scroll to the **Traffic sources** section.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>You'll see three categories: **Direct** (people who typed your URL or had it bookmarked), **Search** (people who found you via Google, Bing, or another search engine), and **Social** (people who clicked a link on social media).</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Each category shows a percentage of total traffic. If Search is low, it may be worth improving your SEO metadata in Site Builder. If Social is low, sharing individual object pages on your social channels can help.</Step>
</Steps>

## Exporting analytics data

<Steps title="How do I export my analytics data to a spreadsheet?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Analytics** and set the date range you want to export.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click the **Export** button in the top-right corner of the page. It appears as a download icon next to the date range selector.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Choose **CSV**. The file downloads immediately and opens in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app. It includes daily page view counts, visitor numbers, and per-object view data for the selected period.</Step>
</Steps>
