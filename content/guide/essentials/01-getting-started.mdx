## Welcome to Vitrine

Vitrine gives every museum (large or small) a professional collection catalogue and a public-facing website. You don't need any technical knowledge to use it. This guide covers everything available on the **Community** (free) and **Hobbyist** (£5/month) plans.

There are four main areas to get to know:

<FeatureGrid>
  <FeatureCard icon="🏛️" title="Objects">
    Your collection catalogue. Add records for every object: title, date, medium, dimensions, description, status, and photos.
  </FeatureCard>
  <FeatureCard icon="🌐" title="Site Builder">
    Your public collection website. Customise the look and feel, write your homepage text, and choose which objects appear prominently.
  </FeatureCard>
  <FeatureCard icon="🎨" title="Customisation">
    Upload your logo, pick your colours, and choose a site template. Your public site looks like yours; Vitrine stays invisible to visitors.
  </FeatureCard>
  <FeatureCard icon="⚙️" title="Settings">
    Manage your account details, see your current plan and usage, and update billing.
  </FeatureCard>
</FeatureGrid>

## Creating your account

<EssentialsDashboardMockup />

<Steps title="How do I sign up?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **vitrine.app/signup**. Enter your name, email address, and choose a password. Click **Create account**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Check your inbox for a verification email from Vitrine. Click the confirmation link to activate your account. (Check your spam folder if you don't see it within a minute.)</Step>
  <Step number={3}>You'll be taken through a short onboarding flow. Enter your museum's name and choose a URL slug. This becomes the permanent address of your public website, for example `mattscoins.vitrine.app`.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Once onboarding is complete, you'll land on your dashboard. The sidebar on the left is your main navigation. You're ready to start adding objects.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Your URL slug is permanent and can't be changed after you create your account, so take a moment to choose something that will still make sense as your museum grows. Keep it short and recognisable: `mattscoins`, `theoldbrickworks`, `localheritagecentre`.
</Callout>

## Understanding the dashboard

When you first sign in, you'll see a summary of your collection at the top: total objects, how many are on display, in storage, on loan, and in restoration. Below that is the full objects list.

The sidebar on the left gives you access to every section of the app:

- **Objects**: your collection catalogue
- **Site Builder**: your public website settings
- **Settings**: your account, plan, and billing

On a desktop, the sidebar is always visible. On mobile, tap the menu icon (three lines) in the top-left corner to open it.

## Community vs. Hobbyist

Both plans give you the same core features. The difference is in scale.

**Community is free** and is a great way to start. You can catalogue up to **100 objects**, attach **1 image per object**, and publish a public collection website right away.

**Hobbyist costs £5/month** and increases the limits to **500 objects** and **3 images per object**. If you're actively cataloguing a growing collection, you'll probably want to upgrade once you approach the 100-object limit.

<Steps title="How do I upgrade from Community to Hobbyist?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Settings** in the sidebar, then click the **Plan** tab at the top of the settings page.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Your current plan and usage are shown here. Click **Upgrade to Hobbyist**.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Enter your card details on the payment page. You'll be charged £5 immediately and then monthly on the same date. You can cancel at any time from the same page.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Your account is upgraded instantly. Your object and image limits increase immediately.</Step>
</Steps>
