## Settings & account

The **Settings** section is where you manage everything about your Vitrine account: your profile details, your museum's name, your current plan, usage, and billing. You can reach it by clicking **Settings** at the bottom of the left sidebar.

## Updating your profile

<Steps title="How do I change my name or email address?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Settings** in the sidebar. The Settings page opens to your profile by default.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click into the **Name** or **Email** field and update the text.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>If you change your email address, Vitrine will send a verification email to the new address. You must click the confirmation link in that email before the change takes effect; until you do, your old address remains active.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Click **Save changes**.</Step>
</Steps>

<Steps title="How do I change my password?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Settings** and click the **Security** tab.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click **Change password**.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Enter your current password in the first field to confirm your identity.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Enter your new password in the second and third fields. Passwords must be at least 8 characters; using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols makes it stronger.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Click **Update password**. You'll remain logged in, but any other active sessions will be signed out automatically.</Step>
</Steps>

## Your plan and usage

The **Plan** tab in Settings shows you your current subscription, when it next renews, and how much of your plan's capacity you've used.

<Steps title="How do I see my plan and current usage?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Settings → Plan**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>At the top you'll see your current plan (Community or Hobbyist), its price, and your next billing date.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Below that is your usage: how many objects you have in your catalogue versus your plan's limit, and how many images you've uploaded.</Step>
</Steps>

<Steps title="How do I upgrade from Community to Hobbyist?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Settings → Plan**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click **Upgrade to Hobbyist**. You'll be taken to a payment page.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Enter your card details. You'll be charged £5 immediately, then monthly on the same date each month.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Your account is upgraded instantly. The higher object and image limits apply right away.</Step>
</Steps>

## Cancelling your subscription

<Steps title="How do I cancel my Hobbyist subscription?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Settings → Plan**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click **Cancel subscription**. A confirmation dialogue will appear explaining what will happen.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Confirm the cancellation. Your account stays active on Hobbyist until the end of the current billing period. If your billing date is in two weeks, you'll have two more weeks of Hobbyist access.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>When the period ends, your account reverts to the free Community plan. Your objects, images, and settings are all preserved; you just lose the higher capacity limits.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
  If you're on Hobbyist and have more than 100 objects when you revert to Community, you won't lose any existing records, but you won't be able to add new ones until your count is back below 100.
</Callout>

## Updating your museum details

<Steps title="How do I update my museum's name?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Settings → Museum**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Update the **Museum name** field. This name appears on your public website and in automated emails sent to visitors.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Click **Save changes**. The new name is reflected on your live site immediately.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  Your museum URL slug (e.g. `mattscoins.vitrine.app`) is set during onboarding and cannot be changed after the fact. If you need to change it, contact support at hello@vitrine.app.
</Callout>

## Deleting your account

<Steps title="How do I permanently delete my Vitrine account?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Settings → Account** and scroll to the very bottom of the page.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click **Delete account**. A confirmation dialogue will appear.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Type your museum name exactly as shown to confirm you intend to delete everything.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Click **Confirm deletion**. Your account, all your objects, all your images, and your public website are deleted permanently.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
  Account deletion is irreversible. Everything (all object records, all images, your site settings, your public website) is permanently erased. There is no way to recover any of it. If you just want a break, consider cancelling your paid subscription instead and keeping the free Community account open.
</Callout>
