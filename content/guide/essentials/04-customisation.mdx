## Customising your public site

The **Site Builder** is where you make your collection website look and feel like yours. You can upload your logo, choose a colour scheme, pick a site template, write your homepage tagline, choose which objects to feature prominently, and add social media links. None of this requires any design knowledge or code.

Everything you change here is applied to your live public site when you click **Publish**. You can preview changes in the right-hand panel before publishing, so there's no risk of accidentally breaking your live site while experimenting.

## Choosing a template

Vitrine offers three site templates, each with a different layout and typographic character. All three are available on Community and Hobbyist at no extra cost.

- **Minimal**: clean, spacious, and uncluttered. Great for intimate or personal collections.
- **Dramatic**: bold typography and strong contrast. Suited to collections where impact matters.
- **Archival**: a structured, document-like feel. Well-suited to heritage and historical collections.

<Steps title="How do I change my site template?">
  <Step number={1}>Click **Site Builder** in the left sidebar, then click the **Appearance** tab at the top of the page.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>You'll see three template options displayed as previews. Click one to select it; the preview panel on the right will update to show how your content will look.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>When you're happy with the choice, click **Publish** in the top-right corner to push the new template to your live site.</Step>
</Steps>

## Uploading your logo

<Steps title="How do I add my museum's logo?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Site Builder → Appearance**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Find the **Logo** section. Click the upload area or drag your logo file onto it.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Use a PNG or SVG file for best results, ideally at least 200 pixels wide. If you have a version with a transparent background, use that; it'll sit cleanly against whatever background colour your template uses.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Once uploaded, a preview shows how it'll appear in the site header.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Click **Publish** to save and apply the change.</Step>
</Steps>

## Setting your accent colour

The accent colour appears throughout your public site: on buttons, links, and highlight elements. By default it's amber, but you can change it to match your museum's brand identity.

<Steps title="How do I change my site's accent colour?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Site Builder → Appearance**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Find the **Accent colour** control. Click the colour swatch to open the colour picker.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Drag the picker to your chosen colour, or type a specific hex code directly into the text field (e.g. `#1a3c6e` for a deep navy blue).</Step>
  <Step number={4}>The preview panel updates live, so you can see the effect before committing.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>Click **Publish** to apply the change to your live site.</Step>
</Steps>

## Writing your homepage tagline

The tagline is the headline that greets visitors at the top of your homepage, above the featured objects. It's the first thing people read, so make it count.

<Steps title="How do I write my homepage tagline?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Site Builder → Content**.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Find the **Tagline** field. Write a short, memorable line that captures what your museum or collection is about. One sentence is usually right, for example: "Exploring 2,000 years of West Midlands heritage."</Step>
  <Step number={3}>In the **Description** field below the tagline, you can optionally write a sentence or two that expands on it: who you are, what you collect, or what makes your collection interesting. This appears as a subheading on the homepage.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>Click **Publish** to save your changes.</Step>
</Steps>

## Featuring objects on your homepage

Your homepage prominently displays a small selection of objects from your collection. You choose which ones appear here; they act as a shop window for the rest of your catalogue.

<FeaturedObjectsMockup />

<Steps title="How do I choose which objects appear on my homepage?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Site Builder → Content**, then scroll down to the **Featured objects** section.</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Click **+ Add featured object** and start typing an object title. Matching results from your catalogue appear as you type; click one to add it.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>You can feature up to 6 objects at a time. They display on your homepage in the order they appear in this list.</Step>
  <Step number={4}>To reorder them, drag the ⠿ handle on the left of each row up or down.</Step>
  <Step number={5}>To remove an object from the homepage (without affecting it in your catalogue), click the × on the right side of its row.</Step>
  <Step number={6}>Click **Publish** to save your changes.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Feature objects that are visually striking or that have a compelling story: these are the pieces that will draw visitors deeper into your collection.
</Callout>

## Adding social media links

<Steps title="How do I link to my museum's social media profiles?">
  <Step number={1}>Go to **Site Builder → Content**, then scroll to the **Social links** section (or click the **Social links** tab at the top).</Step>
  <Step number={2}>Paste the full URL of each social profile you want to link to. Instagram, Facebook, X (Twitter), and YouTube are all supported.</Step>
  <Step number={3}>Click **Publish**. Icons for each linked social network will appear in the footer of your public website.</Step>
</Steps>
